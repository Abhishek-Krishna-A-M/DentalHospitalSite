Privacy Policy

Last Updated: January 2025

At BrightSmile Dental Hospital, we are committed to protecting your privacy and maintaining the confidentiality of your personal and health information. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit our facility or use our services.

Information We Collect

Personal Information

We collect personal information necessary to provide dental care services, including:

  • Name, address, phone number, and email address
  • Date of birth and social security number (for identification and insurance purposes)
  • Emergency contact information
  • Insurance information and payment details
  • Employment information (when relevant for treatment or payment)

Health Information

We collect health information essential for providing quality dental care:

  • Medical and dental history
  • Current medications and allergies
  • Treatment records and clinical notes
  • Diagnostic images (X-rays, photographs)
  • Lab results and test findings
  • Treatment plans and progress notes

Website Information

When you visit our website, we may collect:

  • Browser type and version
  • IP address and location data
  • Pages visited and time spent on site
  • Referral source
  • Information submitted through contact forms

How We Use Your Information

Treatment

We use your health information to:

  • Provide dental care and treatment
  • Coordinate care with other healthcare providers
  • Maintain accurate medical records
  • Monitor treatment progress and outcomes
  • Provide emergency care when necessary

Payment

We use your information for billing and payment purposes:

  • Process insurance claims and payments
  • Collect payment for services rendered
  • Verify insurance coverage and benefits
  • Manage payment plans and financing

Healthcare Operations

We may use your information for:

  • Quality assurance and improvement
  • Staff training and education
  • Business management and administration
  • Legal and compliance requirements
  • Appointment scheduling and reminders

Information Sharing and Disclosure

With Your Authorization

We will share your information with third parties only with your written authorization, except as described in this policy or as permitted by law.

For Treatment

We may share your health information with:

  • Other healthcare providers involved in your care
  • Laboratories and imaging centers
  • Specialists and referral providers
  • Emergency personnel when necessary

For Payment

We may share information with:

  • Insurance companies and benefit plans
  • Claims processing organizations
  • Billing services and collection agencies
  • Financial institutions for payment processing

As Required by Law

We may disclose information when required by:

  • Court orders or legal proceedings
  • Public health authorities
  • Government agencies for oversight activities
  • Law enforcement in specific circumstances
  • Reporting requirements for communicable diseases

Your Privacy Rights

Right to Access

You have the right to request access to your health information. We will provide copies of your records within 30 days of your request, subject to certain limitations and fees for copying costs.

Right to Amendment

You may request amendments to your health information if you believe it is incorrect or incomplete. We will review your request and make appropriate changes when warranted.

Right to Restriction

You may request restrictions on how we use or disclose your health information. While we will consider your requests, we are not required to agree to all restrictions.

Right to Alternative Communication

You may request that we communicate with you about your health information in a specific way or at a specific location to protect your privacy.

Right to Accounting

You have the right to request an accounting of certain disclosures of your health information made by our practice.

Right to Notification

You have the right to be notified if your protected health information is breached or compromised.

Data Security

Physical Safeguards

We maintain physical security measures including:

  • Locked filing cabinets and secure storage areas
  • Restricted access to areas containing patient records
  • Secure disposal of documents containing personal information
  • Employee access controls and identification badges

Technical Safeguards

Our technical security measures include:

  • Encrypted data transmission and storage
  • Secure computer networks and firewalls
  • Regular software updates and security patches
  • User authentication and access controls
  • Regular security audits and monitoring

Administrative Safeguards

We implement administrative controls such as:

  • Employee training on privacy and security
  • Written policies and procedures
  • Regular risk assessments
  • Incident response procedures
  • Business associate agreements

Data Retention

We retain patient records in accordance with state and federal regulations and professional guidelines. Generally, adult patient records are maintained for a minimum of seven years after the last date of service, while pediatric records are kept until the patient reaches the age of majority plus the statutory period.

Certain records may be retained longer based on legal requirements or the nature of the treatment provided.

Cookies and Website Analytics

Our website may use cookies and similar technologies to enhance your browsing experience. Cookies are small text files stored on your device that help us remember your preferences and improve site functionality.

Types of Cookies We Use

  • Essential Cookies: Necessary for basic website functionality
  • Analytics Cookies: Help us understand how visitors use our website
  • Functional Cookies: Remember your preferences and settings

You can control cookie settings through your browser preferences. However, disabling certain cookies may affect website functionality.

Third-Party Services

We may use third-party services to support our operations, including:

  • Payment processing services
  • Email communication platforms
  • Appointment scheduling systems
  • Website hosting and analytics
  • Electronic health record systems

These service providers are required to maintain appropriate security measures and use your information only for the purposes we specify. We ensure all third-party partners comply with applicable privacy regulations.

Marketing Communications

We may use your contact information to send you:

  • Appointment reminders and confirmations
  • Important health information and tips
  • Information about our services and promotions
  • Practice updates and announcements

You can opt out of marketing communications at any time by contacting us or using the unsubscribe link in our emails. However, you will continue to receive essential communications related to your treatment and appointments.

Children's Privacy

We are committed to protecting the privacy of minors. For patients under 18 years of age:

  • Parental or guardian consent is required for treatment
  • Parents/guardians have the right to access their child's health information
  • We comply with special requirements for minors' health information
  • Certain confidential services may be provided to minors as permitted by law

Changes to This Privacy Policy

We reserve the right to modify this Privacy Policy at any time. Changes will be effective immediately upon posting the updated policy on our website. We will notify patients of significant changes through:

  • Posted notices in our office
  • Email notifications (if you have provided your email address)
  • Updates on our website

Your continued use of our services after changes are posted constitutes acceptance of the revised Privacy Policy.

Complaints and Reporting

If you believe your privacy rights have been violated, you have the right to:

  • File a complaint with our Privacy Officer
  • Contact the Department of Health and Human Services
  • Report concerns to relevant state licensing boards

We will not retaliate against you for filing a complaint or exercising your privacy rights.

Note: This Privacy Policy complies with applicable healthcare privacy laws including HIPAA (Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act) and state privacy regulations.
